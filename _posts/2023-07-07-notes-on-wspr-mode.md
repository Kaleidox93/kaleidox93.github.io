WSPR (Weak Signal Propagation Reporter) is a digital communication mode used in amateur radio. It is designed for sending and receiving very low-power signals, typically in the milliwatt range, over long distances using radio waves. WSPR enables the study of radio propagation and provides valuable data for understanding how signals propagate under various conditions.

WSPR operates by transmitting short data packets containing the station’s call sign, location, and power level on a specific frequency. These transmissions are received by other WSPR stations, and the data is collected and uploaded to online databases such as WSPRNet. WSPRNet is a network of servers that receive and aggregate the data from participating stations worldwide, allowing for the analysis of propagation patterns and the monitoring of signal strengths and path losses.

Grafana is a powerful open-source software used for visualizing and analyzing data from various sources. It offers flexible and customizable dashboards that can display real-time or historical data in a visually appealing manner. In the context of WSPR, Grafana can be used to create visual representations of propagation data collected from WSPRNet. This allows radio amateurs and researchers to observe trends, track changes in signal strengths, and gain insights into RF propagation characteristics.

Weather balloons are often employed in radio experiments to measure atmospheric conditions and monitor propagation phenomena. These balloons carry payloads equipped with various sensors and radio equipment, including WSPR transmitters. By launching these weather balloons equipped with WSPR transmitters, researchers can obtain valuable data on atmospheric conditions, wind patterns, and radio signal propagation. The collected data can help in understanding how weather conditions affect radio waves and provide insights into the behavior of RF propagation in different atmospheric layers.
