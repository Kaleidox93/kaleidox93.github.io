Timothy Leary’s 8 circuit model of consciousness is a conceptual framework that outlines different stages or circuits of human consciousness and evolution. It provides a map for understanding the potential development and expansion of human awareness. The model suggests that each circuit represents a distinct level of consciousness and corresponds to specific psychological, neurological, and experiential states.

Here is a brief overview of Timothy Leary’s 8 circuit model:

**1. The Bio-Survival Circuit:**

  
This is the first and most basic circuit, associated with the survival instincts and the basic drives for food, shelter, and reproduction. It is primarily concerned with self-preservation and meeting physiological needs.

**2. The Emotional Circuit:**

  
The second circuit involves the development of emotional responses, bonding, and the formation of social connections. It encompasses the experience of emotions such as love, fear, and anger, and the development of social and interpersonal skills.

**3. The Symbolic Circuit:**

  
The third circuit involves the development of language, symbols, and the ability to think in abstract terms. It is associated with the formation of beliefs, cultural conditioning, and the capacity for conceptual thinking and reasoning.

**4. The Social-Sexual Circuit:**

  
The fourth circuit relates to the exploration of social roles, sexual identity, and the dynamics of power and dominance within social structures. It involves the development of complex social hierarchies and the pursuit of sexual pleasure and reproduction.

**5. The Neurosomatic Circuit:**

  
The fifth circuit represents a shift towards the exploration of inner experiences and altered states of consciousness. It involves the development of somatic and kinesthetic awareness, as well as the exploration of non-ordinary states of consciousness through practices such as meditation, breathwork, and psychedelic experiences.

**6. The Neuroelectric Circuit:**

  
The sixth circuit is associated with the development of higher cognitive functions, including enhanced creativity, problem-solving abilities, and the capacity for abstract thinking. It encompasses the exploration of alternate realities, expanded perception, and the recognition of interconnectedness.

**7. The Neurogenetic Circuit:**

  
The seventh circuit represents a profound shift in consciousness, characterized by the experience of unity, transcendence of the ego, and a deep sense of cosmic interconnectedness. It involves the recognition of one’s place in the larger cosmic order and the exploration of spiritual and mystical experiences.

**8. The Neuroatomic Circuit:**

  
The eighth circuit represents the highest level of consciousness, characterized by the transcendence of ordinary human limitations and the potential for radical transformation. It is associated with the exploration of non-dual states, direct spiritual realization, and the realization of one’s true nature beyond the boundaries of the physical body.

It’s important to note that Leary’s 8 circuit model is a conceptual framework rather than a rigid scientific theory. It provides a way to understand the potential progression and expansion of human consciousness, but its categorizations should be viewed as metaphorical rather than literal.

Leary’s model offers a map for individuals to explore their own consciousness, understand their current level of awareness, and potentially transcend the limitations of their current circuitry through various means, such as meditation, psychedelic experiences, and spiritual practices. It encourages personal growth, self-awareness, and the pursuit of expanded states of consciousness as a means of personal and collective evolution.
