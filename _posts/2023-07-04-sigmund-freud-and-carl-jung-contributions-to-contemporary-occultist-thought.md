Both Sigmund Freud and Carl Jung have had a significant impact on contemporary occultism, including their ideas on dreams, the unconscious mind, and the collective unconscious. These psychological concepts have found resonance and integration within modern occult systems, including Thelema and chaos magick. Let’s explore this influence and overlap in more detail:

1\. Freud and Dreams:

  
Freud proposed that dreams are a gateway to the unconscious mind, containing hidden desires, repressed thoughts, and symbolic representations of unresolved conflicts. His psychoanalytic approach to dream interpretation emphasized the role of personal unconscious material, such as sexual and aggressive instincts, in shaping dream content. Freud’s work on dreams has influenced contemporary occultism by highlighting the importance of exploring the symbolic and subconscious dimensions of the human psyche.

In Thelema:

  
In Thelema, the exploration of dreams and their interpretation plays a role in understanding one’s True Will, personal desires, and subconscious motivations. Aleister Crowley, the founder of Thelema, recognized the significance of dreams as a source of insight and guidance in aligning with one’s True Will. Thelemites often engage in practices such as dream journaling, lucid dreaming, and ritual work to enhance dream recall, interpretation, and integration into their spiritual journey.

2\. Jung and the Collective Unconscious:

  
Jung expanded on Freud’s ideas and introduced the concept of the collective unconscious, which he believed contains universal, archetypal patterns and symbols shared by all humanity. Jung argued that these collective archetypes are reflected in myths, folklore, and dreams across cultures and have a profound influence on human behavior and spiritual experiences. His work emphasized the idea of individuation, the process of integrating the conscious and unconscious aspects of the self.

In Thelema:

  
Jung’s concept of the collective unconscious resonates with Thelema’s view of universal archetypes and symbols. Thelema recognizes the existence of archetypal forces, such as the Egyptian deities in the pantheon of Nuit, Hadit, and Ra-Hoor-Khuit. These archetypes are believed to represent aspects of the human psyche and serve as catalysts for personal transformation and spiritual evolution. Thelemites may explore these archetypes through ritual, meditation, and visualization to connect with deeper layers of the self and tap into collective spiritual currents.

3\. Contemporary Occultism and Psychological Paradigms:

  
Contemporary occult systems, including chaos magick, have embraced psychological paradigms influenced by Freud and Jung. Chaos magick, in particular, places a strong emphasis on individual belief, personal gnosis, and the manipulation of subjective experience. It draws from a diverse range of psychological models, including aspects of psychoanalysis, to inform its practices.

In Chaos Magick:

  
Chaos magick incorporates psychological techniques, such as sigilization and visualization, to reprogram the subconscious mind and manifest desired outcomes. The psychological concepts of Freud and Jung, including dream analysis, symbolism, and the exploration of the unconscious, can be integrated into chaos magick practices to enhance the efficacy of ritual work, self-transformation, and personal evolution.

Freud and Jung’s ideas on dreams, the unconscious mind, and the collective unconscious have influenced contemporary occultism by providing frameworks for understanding the depths of the human psyche and its spiritual dimensions. These concepts have found resonance and practical application within Thelema and chaos magick, enriching their practices with psychological insights, dreamwork, and the exploration of archetypal forces.
