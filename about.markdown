This website serves no real purpose. It was intended to be a shared online storage for information pertaining to a small group of friends, but that project became something better suited to our application, so I slapped a new domain name on the site, gave it a fresh coat of cheap spray paint and here you go, a bunch of random thoughts that have been prettified by AI writing tools. The subject matter discussed here is one I have more than a passing interest in, but it is not a profoundly significant part of my life, and I am not qualified to give definitive answers to questions. The depth of my knowledge is sufficient for dinner table conversation, but little more. Everything you find here has been written for my own amusement and edification, and it should be taken lightly. There is no shortage of more researched information online and at your favorite library or bookstore, and anyone interested is highly encouraged to investigate on your own.

The site takes its name from Aleister Crowley’s work “Equinox of the Gods,” which is part of his philosophical corpus. The full quote is “We place no reliance in virgin or pigeon; our method is science, our aim is religion”.  In this particular quote, Crowley is expressing the perspective of the religious and philosophical system he developed known as Thelema.

When Crowley states, “We place no reliance on virgin or pigeon,” he is emphasizing that his approach does not rely on traditional or superstitious beliefs. The term “virgin” here likely refers to the idea of purity or innocence associated with religious and cultural norms, suggesting that Thelema does not place importance on such concepts. Crowley’s teachings in Thelema are often associated with a rejection of conventional moral and social restrictions.

As for the mention of the pigeon, it’s important to note that Crowley’s writings are often steeped in symbolism and esoteric references. Without further context, it is challenging to pinpoint the exact significance of the pigeon in this quote. However, it could be interpreted in several ways. Pigeons have been associated with various symbolic meanings throughout history, such as peace, fertility, and communication. It’s possible that Crowley’s mention of pigeons is meant to highlight a rejection of traditional religious rituals or superstitions involving pigeons or associated symbolism.

In the latter part of the quote, Crowley states, “our method is science, our aim is religion.” Here, he is suggesting that Thelema embraces a scientific approach to understanding the world while maintaining a spiritual and religious focus. Crowley often sought to combine elements of mysticism, occultism, and esoteric practices with his interest in science, philosophy, and psychology.

Overall, this quote reflects Crowley’s emphasis on individualism, personal exploration, and the pursuit of spiritual knowledge through a blend of scientific and religious principles, while rejecting traditional moral and social constraints. But this website is named after this quote just because the operators thought it would be a great name for a band.
